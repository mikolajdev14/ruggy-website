## Build mandate

Every public page should feel like a complete Ruggy surface. Use the wordmark, real product copy, clear ordering actions, tactile product imagery, useful supporting content, and a proper footer. The ordering flow stays simple and trustworthy, but it should still carry the visual character of the workshop.

## Character and direction

The system combines soft handmade materials with bold editorial type. Rounded image frames, irregular badges, tiny stitch marks, and oversized section numbers suggest tufting without turning the interface into a craft themed costume. Cobalt is used for primary actions and emphasis. Sunflower yellow is a small optimistic signal. Coral appears only as a rare secondary detail.

## Composition patterns

Landing pages use a compact announcement strip, a clear navigation bar, a generous two column hero, an image led gallery, a numbered process, social proof, FAQ, a strong closing action, and a full footer. Order pages keep the same wordmark, canvas, ink, cobalt actions, soft blue panels, and rounded product cards. Content alternates between warm canvas and powder blue sections to create rhythm.

## Component and usage rules

Use one dominant action per section. Cards have soft borders and subtle depth, never heavy glass effects. Product images use generous rounded corners and may overlap small labels. Buttons are rounded but not pill shaped unless they are tags. Decorative shapes must not compete with copy. Keep body copy direct, friendly, and written in Polish.

## Responsive and accessibility direction

Start with one column layouts and preserve at least forty four pixel touch targets. Decorative labels may rotate only slightly and must settle when reduced motion is requested. Keep visible focus states on every link, button, summary, and form control. Never place meaningful copy inside generated images.
